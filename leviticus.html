Following a post-press encounter between Stacey Clausen and Joe Bird, Leviticus expands into a queer horror narrative where intimacy, perception, and fear begin to collapse into the same space. The film explores desire as something unstable — something that reflects, distorts, and ultimately transforms.

Early critical response frames Leviticus as a restrained but emotionally charged work, with particular attention to its visual language and psychological tension.
