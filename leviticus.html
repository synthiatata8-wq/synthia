Leviticus unfolds not as a conventional narrative, but as a fragmented study of visibility — where identity is continuously constructed, observed, and destabilised under the weight of performance.

FESTIVAL CONTEXT

Prior to its wider release, Leviticus was screened at the Sundance Film Festival, where it entered early critical discourse as a restrained yet visually deliberate work exploring the tension between presence and perception.

Its reception positioned the film within a quieter cinematic register — one defined less by narrative clarity and more by emotional control and visual precision.

CRITICAL RESPONSE

Following its festival debut, Leviticus began circulating within critical spaces as a divisive yet formally precise work, drawing attention for its controlled visual language and emotional ambiguity.

POSTER CAMPAIGN

The official poster campaign functions as an extension of the film’s core language — minimal, constructed, and emotionally restrained, reflecting its ongoing preoccupation with image and identity.
