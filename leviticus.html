CRITICAL ACCLAIM

Following its festival debut, *Leviticus* quickly entered critical discourse as one of the most visually precise and emotionally divisive films of its cycle.

Rotten Tomatoes: Certified Fresh — 96% (based on 46 reviews)

Variety — “A work of aesthetic precision and emotional fragmentation that resists easy interpretation.”
The Guardian — “A restrained yet unsettling study of intimacy and image control.”
IMDb — “Anchored by strong performances and deliberate ambiguity.”
Letterboxd — “Hypnotic and slow-burning, leaving a lingering emotional residue.”
