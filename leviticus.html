LEVITICUS

STARRING STACEY CLAUSEN · JOE BIRD

What began as a controlled press appearance for Stacey Clausen and Joe Bird dissolves into something less visible — a quiet collapse of boundaries between performance, identity, and observation.

Leviticus does not explain its world. It lingers inside it. Between silence and spectacle, the film traces what happens when two young men become aware of being seen — and something else begins to look back.

Set against the aftermath of a media cycle appearance, the narrative unfolds as a fractured study of intimacy under scrutiny. Nothing is stable — not even the relationship between the two figures at its center.

CRITICAL RESPONSE

Early responses describe Leviticus as “a film that refuses clarity, choosing atmosphere over explanation.”

“Haunting in its restraint — it feels less like a story and more like something being observed in real time.”
“A meditation on visibility, desire, and distortion.”

Rather than resolving its tension, Leviticus allows it to persist — as if meaning is always slightly out of reach, just beyond what can be said.
